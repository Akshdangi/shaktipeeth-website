The Divine Legacy of Shaktipeeths


Historical Significance

Shaktipeeths are holy places of cosmic and spiritual power, associated with the story of Sati and Lord Shiva. When Lord Shiva carried the body of Sati in extreme grief, Lord Vishnu used his Sudarshan Chakra to dismember the body into 51 pieces, which fell at different locations across the Indian subcontinent.


Shakti Peethas are sacred sites in Hinduism that are believed to be infused with the divine energy of the Goddess Shakti. They are significant because they mark the places where the body parts of Sati fell after she immolated herself.

Significance of Shakti Peethas

Pilgrimage destinations

Devotees visit Shakti Peethas to seek blessings, solace, and spiritual awakening.

Centers of devotion


Shakti Peethas are places of great spiritual importance and are centers of devotion and pilgrimage.
Story of Sati and Shiva The story of Shakti Peethas is intertwined with the tragic tale of Sati's self-immolation and Shiva's grief and rage.

How did Shakti Peethas come to be?


According to legend, Lord Vishnu used his Sudarshana Chakra to divide Sati's body into 51 pieces. The pieces fell on earth, which turned into 51 Shakti Peethas. Each Shakti Peeth is dedicated to a particular form of Adi Shakti. Some examples of Shakti Peethas: Bimala Temple in Odisha, Maa Taratarini Temple in Odisha, Kamakhya Temple in Assam, and Kalighat Kali Temple in West Bengal.
